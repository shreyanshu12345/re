\chapter{Profile of the Organization}

This chapter provides a structured profile of Hyperface, the fintech company where the internship was undertaken. It covers five essential aspects of the organization: an overview of the company including its history and vision, the organizational structure and departmental roles, the products and services offered, the business partners and financial standing, and the company's societal concerns and professional practices.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Company Overview}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Background and History}

Hyperface is a fintech infrastructure company headquartered in Bengaluru, India, specializing in Credit Cards as a Service (CCaaS) solutions for banks, fintech startups, and digital enterprises. The company provides a comprehensive suite of API-driven platforms that enable financial organizations to design, launch, and manage digital credit card and embedded finance products with speed, security, and scalability. Hyperface acts as a technology bridge between traditional financial institutions and the modern digital economy, enabling its clients to offer competitive and feature-rich credit products without the complexity of building core infrastructure from scratch.

The company was co-founded in 2021 by Ramanathan R.V. and Aishwarya Jaishankar, both of whom bring extensive experience in financial technology, product management, and enterprise software engineering. The founding team identified a significant gap in the Indian market: while demand for digital credit cards and embedded finance products was growing rapidly, most banks and fintech companies lacked the internal capabilities to build and manage the complex technological infrastructure these products require. Hyperface was established to fill this gap by providing a ready-made, configurable, and compliance-ready CCaaS platform.

By 2022, Hyperface had completed a major seed funding round of approximately \$9 million, backed by investors including 3one4 Capital, Global Founders Capital, Better Capital, Flipkart Ventures, and Groww, bringing the total funds raised to over \$10 million. This investment accelerated product development, expanded the engineering team, and strengthened the company's go-to-market capabilities. The company maintains operational offices in Bengaluru, Mumbai, Jaipur, and Chennai.

\subsection{Vision, Mission, and Core Values}

The vision of Hyperface is to become the foundational infrastructure layer for digital credit and embedded finance in India and across emerging markets. The company aspires to create an ecosystem where any bank, fintech, or digital business can launch and manage sophisticated credit products within days rather than months, with full compliance, security, and scalability guaranteed by the platform.

Hyperface's mission is to simplify the complexities of digital credit card infrastructure so that financial institutions and technology companies can focus on delivering exceptional customer experiences and innovative financial products. The company is committed to building open, secure, and scalable financial technology that empowers its partners to compete effectively in the fast-evolving digital economy.

The organization is guided by core values of customer-centricity, transparency, innovation, security, and collaboration. Every product decision is evaluated through the lens of the end customer's experience and financial wellbeing. Security and regulatory compliance are treated as foundational requirements, and cross-functional teamwork is encouraged at all levels to drive product excellence and organizational resilience.

\section{Organizational Structure}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Company Hierarchy and Key Departments}

Hyperface operates as a private limited company under the governance of its founding leadership team. As a growth-stage startup, the organization maintains a relatively flat hierarchy designed to encourage fast decision-making, innovation, and cross-functional collaboration. The company is headed by its co-founders, who provide strategic vision and oversight across all operational areas. Below executive leadership, the company is structured into several key functional departments.

The Engineering and Product Development department is the largest department by headcount and is responsible for designing, building, testing, and maintaining all of Hyperface's software products and API platforms. It is subdivided into specialized teams including frontend engineering, backend engineering, DevOps, quality assurance, and product management. The Research and Development department explores emerging technologies and develops prototypes for next-generation features such as machine learning-based credit scoring and real-time fraud detection.

The Sales and Business Development department manages the full client acquisition cycle, from prospecting and proposals through contract negotiation and account management. The Operations department manages day-to-day execution of platform services, including the OMS (Operations Management System) team, which builds and maintains internal tools and workflows for operations staff. The Human Resources, Finance, and Compliance departments support talent acquisition, financial management, regulatory reporting, and risk management across the organization.

\subsection{The Intern's Role and Department}

During the internship, the intern was placed within the Engineering and Product Development department, contributing to the frontend development of the CBCC-PWA (Co-Branded Credit Card Progressive Web Application) platform as part of the OMS team. The internship was structured to provide hands-on experience in real-world fintech software development, working alongside experienced engineers and product managers on live production systems. The intern worked on React, Redux, and TypeScript-based frontend components, secure API integration, and the implementation of the Live Offer Versioning Workflow, all of which are described in detail in subsequent chapters of this report.

\section{Products and Services}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Core Products}

Hyperface offers a comprehensive suite of software products designed to cover the full lifecycle of digital credit card management and embedded finance operations. Each product is delivered as a configurable, API-based platform integrable with the systems of banks, fintech companies, and digital businesses.

\textbf{Credit Pro} is Hyperface's flagship Credit Card and Credit Line Management Platform. It supports the complete credit card lifecycle from customer acquisition and card issuance through transaction processing, billing, payment collection, and credit risk management. Banking partners can configure custom credit products with specific fee structures, interest rates, billing cycles, and card network integrations covering Visa, Mastercard, and RuPay.

\textbf{Smart Engage} is the Customer Rewards and Engagement Platform that enables banks and co-branded card issuers to create, manage, and optimize loyalty programs and promotional campaigns. It supports cashback programs, points-based loyalty systems, milestone bonuses, and partner merchant offers, with a campaign management module for targeted promotions and A/B testing capabilities.

\textbf{Embed Next} is the Co-Branded Credit Card Integration Platform designed to help digital businesses and consumer brands launch co-branded credit card programs in partnership with banking institutions. It provides turnkey technology infrastructure covering eligibility checking, credit application processing, digital card issuance, and rewards management through Hyperface's API layer.

\textbf{Onboard IQ} is the Digital Onboarding and KYC Management Platform providing a mobile-first onboarding experience for credit card applicants. It integrates with India's digital public infrastructure including Aadhaar-based eKYC, PAN verification, and video KYC, and supports compliance with RBI's KYC and Anti-Money Laundering guidelines.

\textbf{CBCC-PWA} (Co-Branded Credit Card Progressive Web Application) is the primary customer-facing interface for co-branded credit card programs. Built as a Progressive Web Application using React, Redux, and TypeScript, it provides card management, EMI management, customer care, offer management, and rewards modules. As the product the intern directly worked on, the CBCC-PWA is described in greater technical detail in Chapters 3 and 4.

\subsection{Services}

In addition to its software products, Hyperface provides a range of professional and managed services to its banking and fintech partners. API Integration and Onboarding Services guide client teams through the technical integration of Hyperface's platform, from initial discovery through go-live validation. Cloud-Based Fintech Infrastructure Services provide hosting, database management, backup, disaster recovery, and infrastructure security management on behalf of banking partners, delivering enterprise-grade reliability without requiring clients to build and operate the infrastructure themselves.

Compliance and Regulatory Support Services help banking partners meet the requirements of the Reserve Bank of India, including PCI-DSS compliance for payment card data security, KYC and AML obligations, and data localization requirements. Analytics and Business Intelligence Services provide embedded dashboards and custom reporting covering transaction analytics, rewards program performance, and fraud metrics. Developer Support and Sandbox Environment Services assist partner development teams with integrations and testing through access to a non-production replica of the platform.

\section{Business Partners and Financial Overview}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Business Partners}

Hyperface has established partnerships across two primary categories: banking institutions and digital commerce platforms. On the banking side, key partners include IndusInd Bank, Yes Bank, and AU Small Finance Bank, all of which use Hyperface's CCaaS infrastructure to power digital credit card programs. These partnerships demonstrate the company's ability to meet the regulatory and operational requirements of licensed banking entities and serve as reference implementations for future banking partnerships.

On the fintech and digital commerce side, Hyperface has partnered with ixigo for co-branded travel credit cards, PharmEasy for healthcare-focused credit solutions, BankBazaar for streamlined credit card distribution, and super.money for UPI-based credit products. These partnerships collectively provide access to tens of millions of digitally active Indian consumers, validating the demand for co-branded credit infrastructure among India's leading digital platforms. Hyperface also maintains technology and cloud partnerships with major infrastructure providers and payment networks to support multi-network credit card issuance across Visa, Mastercard, and RuPay.

\subsection{Financial Overview and Manpower}

Since its founding in 2021, Hyperface has raised over \$10 million in venture capital funding. The landmark event was a seed round of approximately \$9 million in 2022, supported by 3one4 Capital, Global Founders Capital, Better Capital, Flipkart Ventures, and Groww. The company follows a B2B SaaS revenue model combining platform licensing fees, transaction-based fees that scale with client program volumes, and professional services fees for integration and customization engagements.

Hyperface maintains a workforce of several hundred employees distributed across its Bengaluru headquarters and offices in Mumbai, Jaipur, and Chennai. The workforce is technology-focused, with a significant proportion of employees in engineering, product development, quality assurance, and DevOps roles. The company actively recruits from leading engineering institutions through campus hiring and internship programs, and supports professional development through online learning, conference participation, and internal knowledge-sharing sessions.

\section{Societal Concerns and Professional Practices}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Societal Concerns}

Hyperface's work contributes to the broader goal of financial inclusion in India by making modern credit products accessible to a wider range of consumers and organizations. By enabling banks of various sizes and fintech companies to launch digital credit card programs quickly and cost-effectively, Hyperface helps expand the reach of formal credit to customers and businesses that might otherwise be underserved by traditional banking channels. Co-branded credit cards developed on Hyperface's platform often target specific consumer communities, enabling tailored credit products that match the spending patterns and financial needs of distinct customer groups.

As an organization that processes sensitive financial data on behalf of millions of customers, Hyperface bears significant responsibility for data privacy and ethical data handling. The company maintains strict data governance policies, implements robust security controls, and works to ensure that its clients use customer data in ways that are transparent, consensual, and compliant with applicable regulations. Hyperface supports its banking partners in implementing fair credit assessment, transparent interest rate disclosures, and customer-friendly grievance redressal processes.

As a cloud-native and digital-first organization, Hyperface also contributes to environmental sustainability by reducing reliance on physical infrastructure, paper-based processes, and in-person operations. Digital onboarding, electronic statements, and cloud computing minimize the environmental footprint of the services it enables compared to traditional brick-and-mortar banking models.

\subsection{Professional Practices}

Hyperface's engineering and product teams follow Agile Scrum methodology, with development organized into two-week sprints. Sprint planning, daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives form the backbone of the team's delivery cadence. All code changes are submitted through pull requests and reviewed by senior engineers before merging, with automated CI/CD pipelines enforcing linting, type checking, unit tests, and security scans on every change. Security is integrated throughout the software development lifecycle through threat modeling, automated vulnerability scanning, and regular penetration testing by both internal and external security engineers.

The company emphasizes documentation as a core professional practice, maintaining centralized technical documentation, API specifications, and operational runbooks accessible to all team members. New employees and interns benefit from structured onboarding documentation and mentorship relationships that accelerate productive contribution. Knowledge sharing through internal tech talks, code review participation, and retrospective practices ensures that learning is distributed broadly across the organization rather than siloed in individual teams.
